Welcome to the
Museo del Patrimonio Industriale

Step into the heart of Bologna's industrial past, where you can explore the evolution of the city’s technological innovations. From the Hoffmann kilns in the ground floor gallery to the machines and products that shaped the modern city on the first floor, the museum offers a journey through key moments in Bologna’s industrial history.


Navigate through both floors by clicking on the numbers to dive deeper into each section. Enjoy discovering the stories behind the artifacts as you move through the museum!

TAKE A LOOK AT THE MAPS

GROUND FLOOR:
EARLY INDUSTRY & INNOVATION

The ground floor is set within the Hoffmann kiln gallery, featuring models, machines, and scientific instruments from the Aldini Valeriani collections, Bologna's oldest technical school. These exhibits trace key technological milestones of the Industrial Revolution in Bologna and its industrial development from the 19th to early 20th centuries. Highlights include the history of the Galotti Brickworks, home to the museum, and the introduction of Hoffmann kilns in the late 19th century. Visitors can explore the kiln's structure and learn about brick production, from clay extraction to firing.

FIRST FLOOR:
MODERN INDUSTRY & MECHANICS

This floor showcases iconic products representing Bologna’s industrial evolution, such as the Zamboni-Troncon tortellini machine and the ACMA 713 packaging machine. The exhibits focus on the growth of technical knowledge, production systems, and innovative processes that have established Bologna as a leader in automation and manufacturing. Visitors can delve into the dynamics of industrial development, supported by local institutions that have driven competitiveness and advanced production methods.
